About

Noted graffiti artist John “Prime” Hina of 808 Urban will bring his culturally-grounded, learning process, and work with the youth to gather the moʻolelo (stories) of the various ethnic communities that call KPT home. Through multimedia learning experiences, youth will explore and learn the deeper meanings of the moʻolelo, and co-design a concept for a community mural. The community will then be invited to participate in creating the community mural. The project will culminate with a public unveiling of the mural at a large community event.

In parallel with the mural creation process, Purple Maiʻa will host various workshops and work with youth to learn digital skills to document, and promote this project to the larger community. To that end, students will learn digital photography, web application development and produce projects that will be showcased at the community mural unveiling event.
